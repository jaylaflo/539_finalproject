"The Big 3"

Drake, known for his melodic flows and introspective lyrics, has dominated charts with hits like "Hotline Bling" and "God's Plan." J. Cole, with his thought-provoking storytelling and socially conscious themes, has garnered critical acclaim with albums like "2014 Forest Hills Drive" and "KOD." Kendrick Lamar, often hailed as a lyrical genius, has earned widespread acclaim for albums such as "good kid, m.A.A.d city" and "To Pimp a Butterfly," tackling issues of race, identity, and societal injustice.
While the three rappers have collaborated and shown mutual respect in the past, Kendrick Lamar initiated a rap battle with Drake and J.Cole in his verse on Future's "Like That". The conflict between these rap titans has sparked intense debate and excitement within the hip-hop community, showcasing the enduring influence and cultural significance of all three artists. While fans have differing opinions about who wears the crown, this site was created to explore the accomplishments and discography of each individal artist.
